1) Explanation of search - filter components

  • Score (threshold): The prediction score derived from DIANA microT-CDS algorithm. The higher the miTG score the higher the probability of targeting (measures the potency of each miRNA-gene interaction).
    • Only miRNA target genes (miTGs) with score higher than this threshold are shown.
  • Binding Site: The site on the mRNA, where the microRNAs are bound.
  • Number of Conserved Species (threshold): The number of species in which the binding nucleotides of this target site are conserved.
  • Conserved Species: The species in which the binding nucleotides of this target site are conserved.
  • Binding Type : The number of nucleotides that bind in the first 9nt of the alignment and whether there are any G:U wobble pairs.

2) Default search values

  • Score threshold: 0.85
  • Bind Type: 6mer , 7mer , 8mer , 9mer , 8mer+mismatch , 8mer+wobble
  • Bind Site Regions: UTR3 and CDS
  • Number of Conserved Species (threshold): 1 or more
  • Conserved Species: Any of the available conserved species in the DB

5) Web Application Flow

At the home page we have three possible inputs , and we have to fill one to continue ...

  • Gene (Gene Ensembl ID or Gene Name)
  • Transcript (Ensembl Transcript ID)
  • microRNA (microRNA Name or microRNA ID)

Case 1: Input a Gene and observe the available transcripts that show up. Choose one of them and check the final results of your search.

Case 2: Input a Transcript and direct check the final results of your search.

Case 3: Input a microRNA and observe the available transcripts that show up ,along with extra information. Choose one from the list and check the final results of your search.


6) Results Page - What we observe

  • On the chromosome we observe the location of the current gene , with a red vertical line.

  • Next, we observe the gene, along with its coordinates and arrows indicating the strand (left arrows - minus strand , right arrows - plus strand).

  • Below, we observe the whole transcript along with its coordinates and its regions (3'-UTR , CDS , 5'-UTR).
    • Leaving the mouse onto a region component, the coordinates of this region are shown up.

  • Next, we observe only the part of the transcript, where the microRNAs are bound.
    • Similarly , leaving the mouse onto a region component, the coordinates of that region are shown up.

    The coordinates shown here, right and left of the partial-transcript are the coordinates of the transcript regions that are depicted.

  • Lastly, the microRNAs are displayed as small rectangular components. The color of the rectangular component indicates the score as it is described above.
    • Next to each microRNA component is either its name or its family name with bold letters.

    In case a family name is shown instead of microRNA name, that means in that position, more than one microRNAS belonging to the same family, start their interaction with the current miTG.

  • Leaving the mouse onto a microRNA rectangular component, useful information are shown up.
    • Clicking on a microRNA name , all the microRNAs belonging to the same miR family, as the one that was clicked, are noted with a rectangular.
